positive tests in Boone County</h3>\n<p><a href="https://www.como.gov/health/">Columbia/Boone County Health and Human Services</a> is the official source for information about COVID-19 cases in Columbia.</p>\n<p>Because students, staff and faculty can seek testing from a variety of medical providers in and outside of the county, student case numbers provided to the university are incomplete. Information reported in other counties, and the identities of those who test positive, are confidential. Here in Boone County, health officials are tracking the number of MU students who test positive in the county and those figures will be shared on\xc2\xa0 to provide transparency from the most reliable source about the prevalence of the virus on campus. No such county data is available to distinguish between MU staff and faculty cases versus cases within the greater Boone County population. To provide that information, the university began collecting self-reported data from faculty and staff, which will be shared in aggregate to protect privacy.</p>\n<p>MU is also requesting all students, faculty and staff report individual positive cases to the university so instructors and supervisors can be made aware to ensure resources, information and arrangements to work or learn from home can be made, if the person is able. The information provided will be kept confidential.</p>\n<p>Figures on this page will be updated every weekday at approximately noon.</p>\n\n\n    </article>\n\n\n    <div Close contacts will not be informed of the name or any other identifying information of the person who potentially exposed them due to federal privacy laws.</p>\n<h4><strong>Information about specific cases will not be shared</strong></h4>\n<p>Federal privacy laws prohibit the university from disclosing individual identities and any personally identifying information of individuals who test positive. If you are made aware of someone who has tested positive for COVID-19, you must not share that information.</p>\n<p>The individual who tested positive will be isolated, a case investigator will identify close contacts, and contact tracers will contact close contacts and advise them on quarantine.</p>\n<h4><strong>Factors considered for pivot to all-virtual or remote learning</strong></h4>\n<p>A variety of metrics will be considered for the possibility of escalating or deescalating MU\xe2\x80\x99s response to COVID-19, including but not limited to:</p>\n<ul>\n<li>MU-associated and Boone County active cases.</li>\n<li>Availability of hospital care and isolation space.</li>\n<li>Availability of personal protective equipment (PPE) and cleaning supplies.</li>\n</ul>\n<p>Given the fluidity of the situation, MU is working closely with local and state health departments and MU Health Care to monitor the spread of COVID-19.</p>\n<p>&nbsp;</p>\n\n\n    </article>\n\n\n    <div class="renew-main-grid__sidebar">\n\n        \n    </div>\n                      \n    \n<section class="miz-layer miz-layer--brand miz-cta-layer miz-background-gel--gold">\n    <div class="miz-container">\n        <div class="miz-cta-frame miz-fill--black">\n            <header class="miz-layer__header">\n                <h2 class="miz-cta-title">Student self\xe2\x80\x93reporting</h2>\n            </header>\n            <div class="miz-layer__content miz-cta-content">\n                <div class="miz-cta-content__body">\n                    <p>Students must report a positive COVID-19 test to the MU Student Health Center within four hours of receiving their result.
